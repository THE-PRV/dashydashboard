\section{Introduction}
% =====================================================================
\textbf{Access Review} is Broadridge BPO's web application for \emph{tool-access attestation}.
Periodically (each \emph{review cycle}), every associate is asked to confirm which client
applications and tools they still have access to, and which of those they actually used during
the cycle. Managers track their team's progress, reconcile any access disputes, and adjust who can
use which tool. Oversight roles (GFH, GFH~Delegate, IFH, and Admin) monitor completion across
departments and export the exceptions that need follow-up.

The result is an auditable record, each cycle, that access granted matches access needed --- a core
control for client data protection and regulatory compliance.

\subsection{Key concepts at a glance}
\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.5cm,style=nextline]
  \item[Cycle] A defined attestation period with a start date, end date, and \emph{due date}
        (for example, \emph{Q1 2026 Attestation}, due 16 June 2026). The application always opens on
        the current cycle; you can switch cycles from the cycle picker.
  \item[Client] An external client whose applications your team supports (e.g.\ \emph{Barclays},
        \emph{Marex}, \emph{Natixis}). Client IDs are stored as text to preserve leading zeros.
  \item[Tool] A specific application or service used for a client (e.g.\ \emph{Trade Capture},
        \emph{Risk Engine}, \emph{Compliance Portal}).
  \item[Attestation] Your confirmation, per tool, of (a) whether you still have access and
        (b) whether you used it this cycle.
  \item[Dispute] A tool you flag as \emph{No access} --- you are telling the business that the system
        shows access you do not actually have (or no longer need). Disputes are surfaced to your
        manager and to oversight for reconciliation.
\end{description}

\subsection{Who does what}
\begin{table}[H]\centering\small
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}l>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Role} & \textbf{Primary task in Access Review}\\
\midrule
Associate & Attest your own tools each cycle (used / not used / no access) and submit.\\
Manager & Track direct reports' completion; reconcile disputes; grant, time-box, or revoke access.\\
GFH (Global Functional Head) & Monitor completion for the department(s) you head; add clients/tools.\\
GFH Delegate & Same organisation-wide visibility as an administrator (minus user administration).\\
IFH (In-Country Functional Head) & Monitor completion for your department(s) --- view only.\\
Admin & Organisation-wide oversight, client/tool setup, and the user directory.\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\begin{notebox}
Roles are \emph{additive}. A person can be an associate \emph{and} a manager \emph{and} an oversight
role at the same time. The view you land on is your highest role, and you can switch between the
views you are entitled to (see Section~\ref{sec:navigation}).
\end{notebox}

\section{Getting started}
% =====================================================================
\subsection{Opening the application}
Open a web browser (Microsoft Edge or Google Chrome are recommended) and go to:
\begin{center}\large\texttt{http://10.17.69.188/ClientAppAttestation/}\end{center}
Bookmark this address. The trailing slash matters --- always open the URL exactly as shown.

\subsection{Signing in}
Access Review uses your \textbf{Windows / company sign-in} automatically. In normal use you will
\emph{not} see a login form --- the application recognises your Windows account and takes you
straight to your dashboard.

If your identity cannot be verified (for example, you are not yet registered in the system, or you
opened the site from an unsupported network), you will see the sign-in card in
Figure~\ref{fig:login}. Click \ui{Try again}; if it still fails, contact the dashboard administrator
to confirm your account is set up.

\fig{login-sso.png}{0.62}{The sign-in card. You normally bypass this automatically via Windows
single sign-on; it only appears when your account could not be verified.}{fig:login}

\begin{importantbox}
``We could not verify your sign-in'' almost always means your Windows account is not yet mapped to a
user record in Access Review, or your browser did not pass your Windows credentials. Try the site in
Edge/Chrome on the corporate network first, then raise it with the administrator.
\end{importantbox}

\subsection{The top bar and navigation}\label{sec:navigation}
Every screen shares a top bar (Figure~\ref{fig:cyclepicker}). From left to right it contains:
\begin{itemize}
  \item the \textbf{Broadridge / Access Review} brand mark;
  \item the \ui{Cycle picker} --- shows the current cycle, its due date, and days remaining; click it
        to switch to a past or future cycle;
  \item the \textbf{view tabs} (only shown if you have more than the associate role) ---
        \tab{Associate view}, \tab{Manager view}, \tab{Access}, and, for administrators,
        \tab{Admin Dashboard} and \tab{Users};
  \item a \ui{Search} box (filters the current screen);
  \item a \ui{Theme toggle} (light / dark --- see Section~\ref{sec:theme});
  \item your \ui{Profile menu} (name, ID, and \emph{Sign out}).
\end{itemize}

\fig{assoc-cycle-picker.png}{0.95}{The cycle picker open. Each cycle shows its date range and how
many days remain until the due date; a tick marks the cycle you are currently viewing.}{fig:cyclepicker}

\begin{tipbox}
Switching cycles changes \emph{everything} on the screen to that cycle's data. If your numbers look
unexpected, check which cycle is selected in the picker first.
\end{tipbox}

\subsection{Switching between your views}
If you hold more than one role, use the view tabs in the top bar (or, on the oversight dashboard, the
\ui{Associate View} / \ui{Team View} links in the left sidebar) to move between:
the \textbf{Associate view} (your own attestations), the \textbf{Manager view} (your team), the
\textbf{Access} screen (granting/revoking), the \textbf{Admin Dashboard} (oversight), and the
\textbf{Users} directory (administrators only).

% =====================================================================
\section{Associate view --- attesting your own access}
% =====================================================================
This is the part of Access Review that every user completes each cycle.

\subsection{Your dashboard}
On opening, the associate dashboard (Figure~\ref{fig:assocdash}) greets you and summarises the cycle:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Headline} --- how many tools you still have left to attest before the due date.
  \item \textbf{KPI tiles} --- \ui{Attested}, \ui{Remaining}, \ui{Clients}, and \ui{Days left}.
  \item \textbf{Cycle progress} card --- your overall completion (e.g.\ ``4 / 5, 80\%'') with the
        \ui{Submit attestation} button.
  \item \textbf{Client cards} --- one expandable card per client you have access to, each showing the
        tool count and how many are still pending.
\end{itemize}

\fig{assoc-dashboard.png}{0.98}{The associate dashboard. The amber row is a tool still awaiting an
answer; the last client shows one tool marked \emph{Not used} and one flagged as a \emph{No access}
dispute (with a remark).}{fig:assocdash}

\subsection{Reading the attestation table}
Click a client card to expand it. Each tool is one row with four columns
(Figure~\ref{fig:attesttable}):
\begin{description}[leftmargin=2.7cm,style=nextline]
  \item[Tool] The application name.
  \item[Confirm Access] \ui{True} = ``yes, I do have access to this tool''. \ui{False} = ``no, I do
        not have (or no longer need) this access'' --- this raises a \emph{dispute}.
  \item[Did you use?] \ui{Used} or \ui{Not used} this cycle. This is disabled if you set
        \emph{Confirm Access} to \emph{False} (you cannot have ``used'' a tool you say you cannot
        access).
  \item[Remark] An optional free-text note --- \emph{required} when you flag \emph{No access}.
\end{description}

\fig{assoc-attest-table.png}{0.85}{A single client's tool table. ``Risk Engine'' is confirmed but
\emph{Not used}; ``Reporting Suite'' is a \emph{No access} dispute carrying a remark.}{fig:attesttable}

\subsection{Attesting a tool --- step by step}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Expand the client card.
  \item For each tool, set \ui{Confirm Access}:
        \begin{itemize}
          \item \textbf{Keep it \ui{True}} if you still have (and need) this access.
          \item \textbf{Set it to \ui{False}} if you should \emph{not} have this access. The
                \emph{Did you use?} toggle then greys out, and you must add a remark explaining why
                (see Section~\ref{sec:dispute}).
        \end{itemize}
  \item For tools you confirmed access to, set \ui{Did you use?} to \ui{Used} or \ui{Not used}.
  \item (Optional) Add a \ui{Remark} on any tool to give your manager or auditor context.
\end{enumerate}
Your changes save automatically as you click --- there is no separate ``save'' step. Rows you have not
yet answered are highlighted amber and counted in \ui{Remaining}.

\subsection{Reporting a tool you should not have (a dispute)}\label{sec:dispute}
When you set \ui{Confirm Access} to \ui{False}, click the \ui{Remark} button on that row and explain
the situation --- for example, ``I no longer use this tool, please remove my access.''
(Figure~\ref{fig:remark}). Remarks are limited to 500 characters and are visible to your manager and
to audit.

\fig{assoc-remark-modal.png}{0.7}{The remark editor. Leave the box empty and save to clear an existing
remark. A remark is mandatory for every tool you flag as \emph{No access}.}{fig:remark}

\begin{importantbox}
You cannot submit until \textbf{every} tool you marked \emph{No access} has a remark. The application
tells you how many remarks are still missing.
\end{importantbox}

\subsection{Submitting your attestation}
When every tool has an answer (and every dispute has a remark), click \ui{Submit attestation} in the
Cycle progress card. After submission:
\begin{itemize}
  \item the attestation is \textbf{locked} --- a green banner appears on each client and the toggles
        become read-only (Figure~\ref{fig:submitted});
  \item the \ui{Submit} button changes to \ui{Submitted};
  \item your manager sees your status flip to \emph{Submitted}.
\end{itemize}

\fig{assoc-submitted.png}{0.98}{A submitted, locked attestation. Everything is read-only and the
``submitted and locked'' banner is shown. Only a manager can reopen it.}{fig:submitted}

\begin{notebox}
Made a mistake after submitting? Ask your manager to \ui{Reopen} your attestation
(Section~\ref{sec:reopen}); you can then edit and re-submit.
\end{notebox}

\subsection{Dark mode}\label{sec:theme}
Use the sun/moon toggle in the top bar to switch between light and dark themes
(Figure~\ref{fig:dark}). The choice is purely visual and applies to every screen.

\fig{dark-mode.png}{0.98}{The associate dashboard in dark mode.}{fig:dark}

\section{Manager view --- tracking your team}
% =====================================================================
Managers see the \tab{Manager view} by default. It answers one question: \emph{is my team done, and
what needs my attention?}

\subsection{Team overview}
The manager overview (Figure~\ref{fig:mgr}) shows:
\begin{itemize}
  \item a red \textbf{disputes banner} when one or more reports have flagged access disputes this
        cycle, with an \ui{Export disputes} button;
  \item \textbf{summary tiles} --- \ui{Direct reports}, \ui{Submitted}, \ui{In progress},
        \ui{Not started};
  \item the \textbf{Team completion} table --- every direct report with a progress bar, a tools count,
        and a status badge. Filter with \tab{Everyone} / \tab{Not started} / \tab{In progress} /
        \tab{Submitted}, or search by name or ID.
\end{itemize}

\fig{mgr-overview.png}{0.98}{The manager overview, with the access-disputes banner, status summary,
and the filterable team table.}{fig:mgr}

\subsection{Inspecting a team member}
Click any team member to open the \textbf{Selected member} panel (Figure~\ref{fig:mgrdetail}). It
shows their completion percentage and status, a \textbf{per-client progress} breakdown, and ---
if they reported any --- an \textbf{Access disputes} list with the tool, client, and the associate's
remark.

\fig{mgr-member-detail.png}{0.98}{Member detail. The selected associate is fully complete, but has one
access dispute (``Compliance Portal --- Barclays'') that the manager should reconcile.}{fig:mgrdetail}

\subsection{Reopening a submitted attestation}\label{sec:reopen}
If a report needs to correct an attestation they already submitted, select them and click
\ui{Reopen attestation} (visible only when their status is \emph{Submitted}). Confirm the prompt; the
attestation unlocks and the associate can edit and re-submit.

\subsection{Exporting disputes}
Click \ui{Export disputes} (in the banner) to download a spreadsheet of every disputed tool across
your team --- associate, tool, client, the remark, and contact details --- for reconciliation and
access removal.

\section{Access management --- granting and revoking}
% =====================================================================
The \tab{Access} screen (Figure~\ref{fig:access}) lets managers control \emph{which tools each direct
report can use}. Pick a team member from the left list; their access appears on the right.

\fig{access-overview.png}{0.98}{Access management for a selected report: the \emph{Grant new access}
form on top, and the current \emph{Tool access} table below (note the full-access rows, an
\emph{Open access} row, and a row with an end date).}{fig:access}

\subsection{Granting new access}
In \ui{Grant new access}:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Choose a \ui{Client}, then a \ui{Tool}. (You can only grant tools that belong to \emph{your}
        department.)
  \item Set \ui{Access from} (defaults to today) and, optionally, \ui{Access to} for time-boxed
        access. Leave \emph{Access to} blank for open-ended access.
  \item Choose the \ui{Access type} (Figure~\ref{fig:grantform}):
        \begin{itemize}
          \item \ui{Full access} --- access is active immediately.
          \item \ui{Open access} --- a placeholder for access whose paperwork is still in process.
                It is marked \emph{pending} until you later grant it fully.
        \end{itemize}
  \item Click \ui{Grant access}.
\end{enumerate}

\fig{access-grant-form.png}{0.8}{The grant form with \emph{Open access} selected --- used when the
access request is still being processed.}{fig:grantform}

\subsection{Managing existing access}
In the \ui{Tool access} table, each row offers:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{End date} --- edit the \ui{To} date to time-box a full-access row; click \ui{Clear}
        to make it open-ended again.
  \item \ui{Revoke now} --- ends access today (the row is closed out with today's date).
  \item \ui{Grant full access} --- shown on \emph{Open access} rows; promotes a pending placeholder to
        active, open-ended access.
\end{itemize}

\begin{importantbox}
Access scoping is enforced by department: a manager can only grant or change tools that belong to
their own department. If a tool you expect is missing from the \emph{Tool} list, it belongs to another
department --- ask that department's GFH or an administrator to add it.
\end{importantbox}

\section{Oversight dashboards --- Admin, GFH, GFH Delegate, IFH}
% =====================================================================
Oversight roles open on the \textbf{dashboard} (Figure~\ref{fig:admin}). All four roles share the same
layout; they differ in \emph{how much} they can see and \emph{what} they can do.

\fig{admin-dashboard.png}{0.98}{The Admin Dashboard: an organisation-wide KPI band, then one card per
department with a completion donut and a per-client breakdown.}{fig:admin}

\subsection{Dashboard anatomy}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{KPI band} --- associates, associate-tool combinations, submitted, pending, and the
        number of \emph{at-risk} departments in scope.
  \item \textbf{Department cards} --- each shows the responsible GFH, a completion donut, a status
        badge, the submitted/total count, and a per-client completion breakdown. The card colour and
        status follow the completion thresholds in Section~\ref{sec:statusref}.
  \item \textbf{Search} --- filter the visible departments.
  \item \textbf{Cycle picker, notifications, theme, profile} --- as on every screen. The
        notifications bell (Figure~\ref{fig:notif}) flags departments that are behind as the due date
        approaches.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{How the four oversight roles differ}\label{sec:rolediff}
\begin{table}[H]\centering\small
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}lcccc@{}}
\toprule
 & \textbf{Admin} & \textbf{GFH Delegate} & \textbf{GFH} & \textbf{IFH}\\
\midrule
Departments visible        & All & All & Own department(s) & Own department(s)\\
Title shown                & ``Admin Dashboard'' & ``Admin Dashboard'' & ``\emph{Name} --- GFH View'' & ``\emph{Name} --- IFH View''\\
Add Client / Add Tool      & Yes & Yes & Yes & ---\\
User directory (Users tab)  & Yes & --- & --- & ---\\
Drill-down \& exports       & Yes & Yes & Yes & Yes\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\begin{adminbox}
\textbf{In plain terms:} \emph{Admin} can do everything, including managing user records.
\emph{GFH Delegate} sees the whole organisation and can add clients/tools, but cannot administer
users. A \emph{GFH} (Global Functional Head) manages the department(s) they head and can add
clients/tools there. An \emph{IFH} (In-Country Functional Head) monitors their department(s) but
cannot add clients or tools --- it is a view-only oversight role.
\end{adminbox}

\subsubsection{GFH view (scoped, with setup rights)}
A GFH sees only the department(s) they head and the title reads ``\emph{Name} --- GFH View''
(Figure~\ref{fig:gfh}). The \ui{Add Client} and \ui{Add Tool} buttons are available.

\fig{gfh-dashboard.png}{0.98}{A GFH dashboard scoped to a single department (``International''),
retaining the Add Client / Add Tool buttons.}{fig:gfh}

\subsubsection{GFH Delegate view (organisation-wide)}
A GFH Delegate has the same organisation-wide reach as an administrator and keeps the
\ui{Add Client}/\ui{Add Tool} buttons, but has \emph{no} access to the user directory. The dashboard
is titled ``Admin Dashboard''; the role badge in the sidebar reads ``GFHDelegate''
(Figure~\ref{fig:delegate}).

\fig{gfhdelegate-dashboard.png}{0.98}{A GFH Delegate sees every department, like an administrator. The
sidebar role badge (lower left) distinguishes the delegate from a full administrator.}{fig:delegate}

\subsubsection{IFH view (scoped, view-only)}
An IFH sees only their department(s), titled ``\emph{Name} --- IFH View'', and has \emph{no}
\ui{Add Client}/\ui{Add Tool} buttons (Figure~\ref{fig:ifh}).

\fig{ifh-dashboard.png}{0.98}{An IFH dashboard --- scoped like a GFH, but without client/tool setup
buttons.}{fig:ifh}

\subsection{Drilling into a department}
Click a department card to drill in (Figure~\ref{fig:drill}). You get:
\begin{itemize}
  \item a breadcrumb (\ui{All Departments}) to go back, and a \ui{Filter by client} row;
  \item a department header with the \emph{completion breakdown} bar and donut;
  \item an \textbf{Exception Exports} card --- download \ui{Incomplete submissions} (associates who
        have not finished) and \ui{Access disputes} (tools reported as \emph{No access});
  \item a \textbf{Managers} grid --- each manager's completion. Click a manager to drill further into
        their associates.
\end{itemize}

\fig{admin-drilldown.png}{0.98}{Department drill-down: completion breakdown, the Exception Exports card,
and the managers grid.}{fig:drill}

\subsection{Notifications}
The bell in the top bar opens the notifications panel (Figure~\ref{fig:notif}), which highlights
departments falling behind as the due date nears, plus a cycle reminder. Use \ui{Mark all read} to
clear the badge.

\fig{admin-notifications.png}{0.6}{The notifications panel.}{fig:notif}

\subsection{Adding clients and tools (Admin / GFH / GFH Delegate)}
\textbf{Add a client.} Click \ui{Add Client}, enter a \ui{Client ID} (text --- leading zeros are
preserved, e.g.\ ``0039'') and a \ui{Client Name}, then \ui{Add Client}
(Figure~\ref{fig:addclient}).

\fig{admin-add-client.png}{0.55}{The Add Client dialog.}{fig:addclient}

\textbf{Add a tool.} Click \ui{Add Tool}, choose the \ui{Client} and the \ui{Department} the tool
belongs to, type the \ui{Tool Name}, then \ui{Add Tool} (Figure~\ref{fig:addtool}). The tool then
becomes available for managers in that department to assign.

\fig{admin-add-tool.png}{0.55}{The Add Tool dialog. The department determines which managers can later
grant the tool.}{fig:addtool}

\section{User directory (Administrators)}
% =====================================================================
Administrators can open the \tab{Users} tab for a read-only directory of everyone in the system
(Figure~\ref{fig:users}): associate ID, full name, user name, department, and manager. Use the search
box to filter by name, ID, user name, department, or manager.

\subsection{Editing a user}
Click the pencil icon on a row to open \ui{Edit User} (Figure~\ref{fig:useredit}). You can update the
first/last name, user name, department, manager, and e-mail address, then \ui{Save}.

\fig{users-edit-modal.png}{0.62}{The Edit User dialog.}{fig:useredit}

\begin{importantbox}
Changing a user's \ui{Manager} immediately moves them into that manager's team for tracking and access
management. Changing the \ui{Department} affects which tools can be granted to them.
\end{importantbox}

\subsection{Glossary}
\begin{description}[leftmargin=2.8cm,style=nextline]
  \item[Attestation] A user's per-tool confirmation of access and usage for a cycle.
  \item[Cycle] A dated attestation period with a due date.
  \item[Client] An external client supported by the BPO operation.
  \item[Tool] An application/service used for a client.
  \item[Dispute / No access] A tool the user states they should not have; requires a remark.
  \item[Full access] Active access to a tool.
  \item[Open access] A pending access placeholder (paperwork in process), not yet active.
  \item[GFH] Global Functional Head --- owns oversight (and setup) for a department.
  \item[GFH Delegate] An organisation-wide delegate of the GFH function, without user administration.
  \item[IFH] In-Country Functional Head --- view-only department oversight.
  \item[Admin] Full administrator: org-wide oversight, setup, and user administration.
\end{description}

\subsection{Troubleshooting \& FAQ}
\begin{description}[leftmargin=0.4cm,style=nextline]
  \item[\textmd{\textbf{``We could not verify your sign-in.''}}]
        Your Windows account is not yet linked to an Access Review user, or your browser did not pass
        your corporate credentials. Use Edge/Chrome on the corporate network and click \ui{Try again};
        if it persists, contact the administrator.
  \item[\textmd{\textbf{The page loads but shows no data / 404s.}}]
        Make sure you opened the exact URL \texttt{http://10.17.69.188/ClientAppAttestation/}
        (including the trailing slash).
  \item[\textmd{\textbf{I can't click \emph{Submit}.}}]
        Every tool must have an answer, and every \emph{No access} tool must have a remark. The app
        shows how many items still need attention.
  \item[\textmd{\textbf{My attestation is locked.}}]
        You have already submitted. Ask your manager to \ui{Reopen} it, then edit and re-submit.
  \item[\textmd{\textbf{A tool I need to grant isn't in the list.}}]
        Tools are scoped to a department. Ask that department's GFH or an administrator to add it.
  \item[\textmd{\textbf{My numbers look wrong.}}]
        Check the cycle picker --- you may be viewing a different cycle.
\end{description}
